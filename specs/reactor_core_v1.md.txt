# Reactor Core Specification (v1.0)

## 1. Core Geometry & Capacity
* **Type:** Passive Thermal-Hydraulic Bioreactor
* **Volume:** 10,000 Liters (10 m³) per unit
* **Throughput:** 1 ton/day (continuous feed)
* **Operating Temperature:** 60°C – 90°C (maintained via decay heat)

## 2. Fuel Source Assembly
* **Isotope:** Strontium-90 (Sr-90)
* **Form Factor:** Sintered Ceramic Pellets (SrTiO₃)
* **Cladding:** Zirconium Alloy (Zircaloy-4), 0.5mm thickness
* **Array:** Hexagonal bundle, centrally mounted for uniform heat distribution
* **Activity:** 1-5 kCi total load per reactor

## 3. Containment Architecture (Quad-Barrier)
The safety of the RAPL system relies on four independent containment layers:
1.  **Ceramic Lattice:** The Sr-90 is chemically bound in a non-soluble ceramic matrix.
2.  **Zirconium Cladding:** Corrosion-resistant, pressure-sealed metal shell.
3.  **Chemical Trap:** The slurry contains **Biochar (5% w/w)** to adsorb immediate leaks.
4.  **Zeolite Shield:** The reactor vessel is cast from **Clinoptilolite-Impregnated Concrete** (15cm thick), acting as a permanent ion-exchange barrier for any escaping radionuclides.

## 4. Operational Parameters
* **Pressure:** Ambient (Negative pressure ventilation system)
* **Phosphate Recovery Target:** >24% efficiency (via GLDA chelation)
* **Residence Time:** 7-14 days per batch
